Places to Visit

Ranging from the urban sprawl of Mogadishu to the cloud-toped, camel-spotted peaks of the Cal Madow ranges in the north, this country is untouched and undeveloped; a picture of what Africa once was before the onset of mass safari-going.

Muqadishu

Meet-and-Greet at Mogadishu International Airport and secure transfer to our compounded hotel. Welcome refreshment and introductory briefing about the set of rules to observe while travelling in Somalia. Visit to the Somali Youth League Memorial, the Dhagaxtuur Obelisk, and the Monument to the Unknown Soldier.

Kismaayo

Kismayo. Situated in the far south of the country close to the Kenyan border, Kismayo is the capital of Jubbaland, the lushest region of Somalia, and a semi-autonomous region of Somalia.

Bosaso

Bosaso, located in Puntland, Somalia, offers a mix of coastal attractions and cultural experiences for tourists. Key highlights include the beautiful beaches, the nearby Biyo Kulule tourist area, and the city's role as a commercial hub. While Somalia's tourism sector is still developing.

Sa’ad ad-Din Island

The first half of the fabled Zeila Archipelago to make this list is also the most visited of the six islets that lurk just a stone’s throw from the coast of Somaliland off the ancient city of Zeila.

Baidoa

Baidoa or Iscia Baidoa, is a Somali city and the capital of the Southwestern State of Somalia, situated in the south-central bay region of the country, around 250 km northwest of Mogadishu, the capital of Somalia.

Iskushuban

One of the major attractions in the autonomous region of Puntland in the northern reaches of Somalia, the ancient settlement of Iskushuban has crenulated walls of dust-caked stone and beautiful arabesque fortifications from centuries gone by.
